About Paul Pearson

Paul Pearson is a writer, DJ and musician based in southeast Seattle.

His music journalism has appeared in The Stranger, The Seattle Times, The Olympian, Seattle Sound, Popmatters.com and several other online publications. In 2013 Paul became a regular contributor to Treble, a collective music site.

Since 2016 Paul has been the producer and host of Star Time, an R&B history show for Seattle community station Rainier Avenue Radio.

For Microsoft's music properties MSN Music, Zune and Xbox Music, Paul created over 1,000 playlists and programmed more than 30 internet radio stations. He also co-directed and conducted music interviews for The Green Room, Zune's top-rated video podcast.

Paul holds a Bachelor's degree from The Evergreen State College in Olympia, Washington. He also hosted a variety music program, Shrug Festival, on Olympia community station KAOS.

A pianist since age eight, Paul was a member of Olympia bands Rodeo Kill, King Dinosaur and Wonder Woman. He's also performed with Harvey Sid Fisher and Sister Carol.

Paul is a two-time regional winner of the notoriously difficult Rhino Musical Aptitude Test (RMAT), winning the competition in Los Angeles in 2000 and Seattle in 2001.

In 2006 Paul created SB Nation's Seattle Seahawks fan blog Field Gulls, and served as editor through 2007.

Paul lives in the Columbia City district of Seattle with his wife and three children.
